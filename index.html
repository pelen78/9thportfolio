Computer Applications · 9th Grade

End-of-Year
AI Portfolio

Students build a digital reflection of their school year using AI as a thinking and design partner — not a shortcut.

Duration 1 week (5 days)
Deliverable PDF, Doc, or Slides
AI tools Any (free choice)
Covers All subjects
The AI rule: AI helps students organize, improve, and design — but every idea must come from them first. Each section must include a short note: "How I used AI here." This keeps the work authentic and makes the process visible.

Portfolio sections

Section 1
About Me
Who they are today vs. who they were at the start of 9th grade. A photo or AI-generated avatar, 3 words that define their year, and one goal for 10th grade.
AI: Generate an avatar or write a short bio from bullet points.
Section 2
Academic highlights
One highlight per subject (or their favorites). What was the most important thing they learned? What work are they proud of?
AI: Summarize or improve written reflections.
Section 3
Biggest challenge
One hard moment from the year — academic or personal. What happened, how did they handle it, and what did they learn from it?
AI: Use as a writing coach to get unstuck or sharpen the story.
Section 4
My impact
How did they contribute to their class, school, or community this year? Can be small — helping a classmate counts.
AI: Expand a short idea into a full paragraph.
Section 5
Letter to future me
A letter addressed to their 12th grade self. Predictions, advice, and things they never want to forget about this year.
AI: Format as a formal letter while keeping their own voice.
Section 6
AI reflection
The most important section for Comp Apps. What tools did they use? Where did AI help and where did it fall short? What did they learn about working with AI?
AI: Ironically, they can discuss this one with AI too.

Grading rubric

All 6 sections completed 30 pts
Reflections are genuine and detailed 30 pts
AI use is documented and intentional 20 pts
Clean, organized design 20 pts
Total 100 pts

The letters from Section 5 can be saved and "delivered" at graduation — a nice touch if you want to keep the tradition alive. 🎓
