End-of-Year
AI Portfolio

You will build a digital reflection of your school year — as a real HTML website — using AI as your coding and design partner.

Duration4 days
DeliverableHTML file
AI toolsAny (your choice)
CoversAll subjects
The AI rule: You will use AI to write and style your HTML — but every idea, story, and reflection needs to come from you first. In each section, you must include a short note: "How I used AI here." This keeps your work authentic and shows you understand the process.

What you need to submit

You will submit a single .html file that opens in any browser.

You don't need to know how to code. You will use an AI tool (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.) to generate and style your HTML. Your job is to give it your content and keep giving feedback until it looks the way you want.

  • Start by telling the AI: "Build me a personal portfolio webpage with these sections: About Me, Academic Highlights, Biggest Challenge, My Impact, Letter to Future Me, and AI Reflection."
  • Then you need to paste your own content section by section and ask the AI to format it nicely.
  • You can ask the AI to change colors, fonts, or layout until you like how it looks.
  • Save your final page as lastname_portfolio.html and submit it.

Your portfolio sections

Section 1
About Me
Who are you today vs. who you were at the start of 9th grade? You need to include an AI-generated avatar, 3 words that define your year, and one goal for 10th grade.
Evidence to include
  • An AI-generated avatar (describe yourself to the AI and ask it to illustrate you)
  • A short bio paragraph written with AI help
AI: Generate your avatar or turn your bullet points into a short bio.
Section 2
Academic highlights
You will write one highlight per subject. What was the most important thing you learned? What work are you proud of?
Evidence per subject (2–3 each)
  • A graded assignment, quiz, or test score
  • A photo, scan, or screenshot of your work
  • A written reflection or project description
AI: Summarize or improve your written reflections per subject.
Section 3
Biggest challenge
You need to pick one hard moment from this year — academic or personal. What happened? How did you handle it? What did you learn from it?
Evidence to include
  • A before/after example (draft vs. final, low grade vs. improvement)
  • A short written reflection in your own words
AI: Use it as a writing coach when you don't know how to start.
Section 4
My impact
How did you contribute to your class, school, or community this year? It can be small — helping a classmate counts.
Evidence to include
  • A photo, email, or message that shows your contribution
  • A description of what you did and the outcome
AI: Use it to expand a short idea into a full paragraph.
Section 5
Letter to future me
You will write a letter to your 12th grade self. Include your predictions, advice, and things you never want to forget about this year.
Evidence to include
  • The full letter, formatted as a real letter (date, greeting, signature)
  • At least one specific memory or moment from this year
AI: Ask it to format your ideas as a letter while keeping your own voice.
Section 6
AI reflection
What tools did you use to build this page? Where did AI help you and where did it fall short? What did you learn about working with AI?
Evidence to include
  • A list of every AI tool you used and what you used it for
  • One example of a prompt that worked well and one that didn't
AI: You can actually talk this one through with AI too.
